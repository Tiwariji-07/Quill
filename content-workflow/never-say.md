# Never say

Banned phrases. If a draft contains any of these, rewrite the line.

## Hype openers
- "Excited to share..."
- "Thrilled to announce..."
- "Beyond pumped to..."
- "Honored to..."

## Hype phrases mid-post
- "Game-changer" / "game changing"
- "Revolutionary"
- "Cutting-edge"
- "Next-generation" (as a vague modifier)
- "Best-in-class"
- "World-class"
- "Synergy" / "synergies"
- "Leverage" (as a verb when "use" works)
- "Empower" (when not literal)
- "Unlock" (as a metaphor for "enable")

## AI clichés
- "AI is changing everything"
- "The future of AI is..."
- "AI is eating the world"
- "Welcome to the age of AI"
- Any sentence that starts with "In today's fast-paced world..."

## Engagement-bait
- "Comment YES if you agree"
- "Tag someone who needs to see this"
- "DM me for more"
- "Drop a 🙌 in the comments"
- "What's your take? 👇"
- Any closing question framed as a CTA rather than a real question

## Emoji — minimal use, near-zero default

- **Default: zero emoji in a post.** If a post has any, it should be at most one, and only if it adds information the words couldn't.
- Never decorative. Never as a stand-in for emphasis. Never as a section separator.
- Hard ban regardless of context: 🚀 💡 🔥 ✨ 🎯 💪 🙌 👇 🧠 🤖
- Functional emoji acceptable only inside actual checklists or status markers (rare). If in doubt, leave it out.

## Punctuation — no em dashes

- **Never use em dashes (—).** They are the single strongest "this was written by an LLM" tell.
- Use a period, a comma, or parentheses instead. Restructure the sentence if needed.
- En dashes (–) also banned in prose. Plain hyphens (-) only when joining compound words.
- Avoid semicolons in posts. Two sentences read more human than one with a semicolon.

## Structural smells
- Numbered listicles with no narrative ("5 things I learned about X")
- "Here's the thing:" as a transition
- "Spoiler alert:" outside actual spoiler contexts
- "Plot twist:" applied to non-twist content
- Single-word sentences for fake emphasis. Like this. See? Annoying.

## Why these matter

Every banned phrase is a tell that the writer is performing instead of thinking. Vivek's edge is sounding like an engineer who is actually building things — not a content creator who is performing engineering.
