## Post body

Most takes on "AI for engineers" come from people who don't ship code.

Ctrl+Shift+AI is our podcast series, and the people on it actually do.

Every episode, we sit down with an engineer from a different stack, role, or career stage and ask one thing: how did AI actually change the way you work, and what broke along the way.

Not the polished demo. The parts people skip: what they tried, what they dropped, where it surprised them.

First episode is out today.

If you build with AI day to day: what is one task you handed to AI and then quietly took back?
